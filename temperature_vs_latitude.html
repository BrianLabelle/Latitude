Temperature (F) vs. Latitude

The scatter plot that confirms the statement ‘Duh, It gets hotter…” as we approach the equator is scatter plot for the City Latitude Versus Temperature as compared to the other graphs plotted.

The plotted data fills in a concave graph with the apex almost over the equator. It can be clearly seen that the maximum temperatures of the day is at the around the 20 degree latitude ( Northern Hemisphere), which is just NORTH of the equator. This is because during the month of June / July, the sun is directly shining

June / July: Winter south of the equator, summer north of the equator. The sun shines directly on the Northern Hemisphere and indirecetly on the Southern Hemisphere.
